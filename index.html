Component Library

Account

This first component is from Amazon.com. I chose Amazon because I am always on Amazon! In 1995, Amazon.com began as an online bookstore before diversifying its product lines by adding VHSs, DVDs, music CDs, MP3s, computer software, video games, electronics, apparel, furniture, food, toys, and more. They offer a Prime Membership which allows members to enjoy faster shipping times and special sales.

Target Audience

People who shop on Amazon.com:

  • must have a credit/debit card
  • like to comparison shop
  • must have access to a computer with an internet connection
  • do not like to go to the mall
  • are digital natives, someone that is comfortable shopping online
  • are avid readers and Kindle owners
  • males or females

Client Goals & User Needs

Amazon's goals are simple. They want to make money and have users sign up for Prime memberships that will expand their customer base. User needs are to be able to find a product quickly, compare prices, and have it shipped to their house fast.

Amazon also wants to make sure that the user has as much input and freedom over their account information without having to call for support. Amazon does a great job of this by having several categories for users to edit. Some websites have the user call customer service if they need to change their name, address, or password. With Amazon's extensive member management tools, the user can do most everything on their own.

Critique

The account information is placed in the header where it is easy to find. The user can check on account info, membership info, wish lists, and their shopping cart. Amazon used a scroll over tab feature to show these different sections. When you scroll over the tabs, a list of information drops down. Scrolling over "Your Account" (shown in the image above), drops down in a list for you to choose what you want to do. The text is a very dark gray, which is professional and mature, while sub-categories are lighter gray (practical and quality) and smaller font. This shows hierarchy. To the left is a "buy it again" section. This shows the last few items purchased on this account to make it easier for re-purchases or to keep track of when and what has been purchased. The text for this is a bright blue to differentiate from the account information. The price is written in red to make it pop. The "Buy it Again" text, as well as part of the "prime" icons, are orange which promote action.

Blog

This is a blog section from FileHippo.com. They offer the latest free downloadable software, updates and drivers.

Target Audience

People who use this website are:

  • computer users
  • searching for a specific piece of software
  • need an update
  • searching for the latest and best
  • want free and safe software
  • looking to share their opinions or knowledge
  • want the latest tech news
  • need drivers for their computers or hardware

Client Goals & User Needs

FileHippo's goals are to offer the latest, safest, free downloadable software, drivers and updates as well as technical news and opinions. The user's goals are to find that information.

Critique

This blog/news section is just that, an HTML section. It's located to the right of the CTA. As your eye follows the page in a left to right fashion, this is the second place your eye stops. The title is a bold dark gray font (which shows maturity and professionalism) while the different article titles are not bold in a lighter shade of gray (which shows practicality and quality). By doing this, hierarchy is shown. The use of a newspaper icon next to the title, (in a trustworthy blue), shows the user that this is the section for news. The blog section also has thumbnail pictures of what the article is about to draw you in even further. By teasing you with a picture of an angry Droid, you want to find out what is making him angry.

Contact

This contact page is from Walgreens. I chose it because of it's detail. With the many sections and sub-sections, you are sure to find exactly what you are looking for or at least who to talk to. They even have a live chat feature if you are not able to obtain the information you need.

Target Audience

The target audience for Walgreens would include:

  • customers who want to refill or set up automatic refills for a prescription
  • members of the "Balance Rewards" program
  • travelers that want to find the closest store
  • people who want to develop film or upload pictures
  • sick people looking for the in-house clinic
  • people needing health information or advice

Client Goals & User Needs

Walgreens wants to increase their customer numbers and provide an easy way to order/re-order medication when the customer is sick. They also want to provide important information about the locations of their stores and walk-in clinics. Users would like to shop from home when they are not well or injured and they want to be able to find the closest store in case of an emergency.

Critique

The contact information is located in the footer under customer service, which is a very common place for this component. Once you open the contact option, (see image above) there are categories to guide you to the right place. Walgreens has eleven different categories of contact. Within these eleven categories are between 3 and 8 sub-categories. When the user clicks on a sub-category, another 3 to 6 categories drop down. This is one the most specific contact pages I have ever seen.

All of these categories and sub-categories are aligned into three columns. This makes it easier to follow and look through each list. I would like this contact page more if the categories were alphabetized. Each title is a black font that, in my opinion, should be bold, but is not. The subs are a bright blue which promote trust.

CTA

This Call to Action is from the snapfish.com. Snapfish offers user an account to upload pictures from any computer with an internet connection. The user can also share their "albums" with anyone who has a computer and internet connection. Families who have relatives in another state or country would be able to share their photographs online. College students could keep up with friends. Travelers could upload photos while on vacation without having to wait until they get home to show off. Snapfish also offers products on which to print your photos such as coffee mugs, calendars, or mouse pads.

Target Audience

Snapfish.com's target audience is:

  • "shutterbugs" or amateur photographers
  • long-distance families
  • frequent travelers
  • college students
  • crafters

Client Goals & User Needs

Snapfish does not charge for their upload and album saving service. They do charge for printing and mailing the pictures to the user. Snapfish wants to bring in customers that upload photos and with the use of a creative CTA, have them buy products or prints. Users enjoy the free membership and photo "storage" and will most likely purchase prints and have them mailed. This means the photographer never has to leave his home.

Critique

The CTA is under the header. Snapfish shows three different CTAs using a scroll feature. After 10-15 seconds the next CTA scrolls by. One is a special sale or promotion for the week/month (i.e. Father's Day, Christmas...). The next two show specific products, whether they are on sale or just a craft suggestion for your photos. This reminds the user of what Snapfish has to offer. The scroll titles are written in trustworthy blue while the active tabs is black and underlined. This is so the user can tell at which CTA they are looking. A yellow-orange button is used to take the user to that specific page. This color brings about feelings of happiness and warmth which is exactly how you should feel when looking through your memories. Yellow is often used as "shop here" buttons

Special offer codes are written in a bold, black font the make the user focus on this info. Snapfish uses actual photos on their products to demonstrate how your pictures could be used.

Features

This Features page is from the Apple website. Their website is minimal. This is one of four components on the homepage; navigation, CTA, features, and footer. They do not have advertisements or asides. They are such an iconic brand, it is not necessary to clutter up the site.

Target Audience

People that come to Apple.com are:

  • current Apple users looking for the latest products
  • people who want to switch over to Mac
  • users who need updates or service for their devices

Client Goals & User Needs

Apple wants to promote and sell their latest products. They want to give the user a site to come for updates and service. Users need a safe and secure place to come for the latest Apple products or service and updates for their current devices.

Critique

As I previously stated, the Apple website is a sparse. With only four components, the user uses the navigation bar for most of their information. Apple's background color choice is white, (clean and sterile), with grays and black, (classic and powerful). The features component consists of four thumbnails and a link written in a calming blue beneath them. This is basically the only color on the page. The combination of the minimal feel of the site and the clean, crisp colors used makes the user feel confident about this company.

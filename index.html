Component Library

About

I chose an "about" component from a floral shop.

Target Audience

The people who will use this site are young adults to middle aged, maybe 25 - 50 years old. Digital natives or well-versed in computer shopping, trust the security. They are busy people that don't have time to go to a flower shop to purchase flowers. Possibly forgetful husbands who need a quick gift for a forgotten birthday or anniversary. The users could have family, friends, or loved ones who live in another state.

Client Goals & User Needs

This website's "about" component wants to give the background of the company as well as information about how easy the site is to use and a history of their great customer service.

The user will look at this part of the site when he has doubts about the legitimacy or history of the company. He will find answers to questions about ordering flowers and customer service.

Critique

The fresh white background makes the site feel clean, airy and lightweight. Nothing is too heavy or overwhelming to make the user feel confused. There are splashes of a lively bright green which is a sign of Spring grass and foliage. The text is a san-serif font in a neutral gray expressing classic, quality and timeless design.

Footer

This Call to Action is from the snapfish.com. Snapfish offers user an account to upload pictures from any computer with an internet connection. The user can also share their "albums" with anyone who has a computer and internet connection. Families who have relatives in another state or country would be able to share their photographs online. College students could keep up with friends. Travelers could upload photos while on vacation without having to wait until they get home to show off. Snapfish also offers products on which to print your photos such as coffee mugs, calendars, or mouse pads.

Target Audience

Snapfish.com's target audience is:

  • "shutterbugs" or amateur photographers
  • long-distance families
  • frequent travelers
  • college students
  • crafters

Client Goals & User Needs

Snapfish does not charge for their upload and album saving service. They do charge for printing and mailing the pictures to the user. Snapfish wants to bring in customers that upload photos and with the use of a creative CTA, have them buy products or prints. Users enjoy the free membership and photo "storage" and will most likely purchase prints and have them mailed. This means the photographer never has to leave his home.

Critique

The CTA is under the header. Snapfish shows three different CTAs using a scroll feature. After 10-15 seconds the next CTA scrolls by. One is a special sale or promotion for the week/month (i.e. Father's Day, Christmas...). The next two show specific products, whether they are on sale or just a craft suggestion for your photos. This reminds the user of what Snapfish has to offer. The scroll titles are written in trustworthy blue while the active tabs is black and underlined. This is so the user can tell at which CTA they are looking. A yellow-orange button is used to take the user to that specific page. This color brings about feelings of happiness and warmth which is exactly how you should feel when looking through your memories. Yellow is often used as "shop here" buttons

Special offer codes are written in a bold, black font the make the user focus on this info. Snapfish uses actual photos on their products to demonstrate how your pictures could be used.

CTA

This Features page is from the Apple website. Their website is minimal. This is one of four components on the homepage; navigation, CTA, features, and footer. They do not have advertisements or asides. They are such an iconic brand, it is not necessary to clutter up the site.

Target Audience

People that come to Apple.com are:

  • current Apple users looking for the latest products
  • people who want to switch over to Mac
  • users who need updates or service for their devices

Client Goals & User Needs

Apple wants to promote and sell their latest products. They want to give the user a site to come for updates and service. Users need a safe and secure place to come for the latest Apple products or service and updates for their current devices.

Critique

As I previously stated, the Apple website is a sparse. With only four components, the user uses the navigation bar for most of their information. Apple's background color choice is white, (clean and sterile), with grays and black, (classic and powerful). The features component consists of four thumbnails and a link written in a calming blue beneath them. This is basically the only color on the page. The combination of the minimal feel of the site and the clean, crisp colors used makes the user feel confident about this company.

Navigation

Target Audience

People who shop on Amazon.com:

  • must have a credit/debit card
  • like to comparison shop
  • must have access to a computer with an internet connection
  • do not like to go to the mall
  • are digital natives, someone that is comfortable shopping online
  • are avid readers and Kindle owners
  • males or females

Client Goals & User Needs

Amazon's goals are simple. They want to make money, have users sign up for a Prime membership to expand their customer base. User needs are to be able to find a product quickly, compare prices, and have it shipped to their house fast.

Critique

Amazon placed the search bar in the header. Searching for products is the main reason to visit the website. The search bar has very specific drop down menu with almost 40 different categories to search. The search bar itself is the main focus of the header. This shows importance. The search icon is a bright orange color, which stimulates activity, shows action and draws attention. That same orange is used in the logo which ties everything together.

Amazon has another way to search. It is located under the brand logo. When using this search function, you can search by department, which is just as specific. There are 20 categories to choose from here with about half of them focusing specifically on downloads or streaming movies, music, or books. With as much information as Amazon covers, it is all laid out neat and tidy. This makes a more enjoyable visit to the site.
